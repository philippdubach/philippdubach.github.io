Philipp D. Dubach writes about the structural mechanics of artificial intelligence, capital markets, and complex systems. The focus is unit economics, variance, and cross-domain synthesis.

Philipp D. Dubach is a strategy consultant and independent researcher trained at Imperial College London, working at the intersection of quantitative finance, machine learning, and AI economics. Peer-reviewed work — covering prediction market microstructure, volatility regimes, and glycemic modeling — lives on [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/cf_dev/AbsByAuth.cfm?per_id=9538192#form1) and [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/search/q-fin?searchtype=author&query=Dubach,+P+D). The full list is on the [research](/research/) page or [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=-69oS2wAAAAJ).

The site started in 2024 as a personal repository — articles, papers, and [projects](/projects/) worth keeping in quantitative finance and data science. Recent work includes [The SaaSpocalypse Paradox](/posts/the-saaspocalypse-paradox/), [AI Capex Arms Race: Who Blinks First?](/posts/ai-capex-arms-race-who-blinks-first/), and [Pozsar's Bretton Woods III: The Framework](/posts/pozsars-bretton-woods-iii-the-framework-1/2/).

Readers tend to be portfolio managers, CTOs, founders, and researchers who want the mechanics rather than the narrative. Eighty-plus essays since 2024, plus a weekly note on AI and markets.
